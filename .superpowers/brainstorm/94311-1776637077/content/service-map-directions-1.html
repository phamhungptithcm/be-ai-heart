A. Trust-First Catalog

Presents each service like a controlled product module: name, function, and trust signal. Best balance between clear offering and enterprise credibility.

Pros

  • Feels buyer-ready and premium
  • Sub item becomes easier to scan
  • Good for both homepage and services page

Tradeoff

  • Needs tighter copy so each line earns its space

B. Service Ledger

Turns the section into an audit-like operating table. Strongest “governed platform” signal, but less visually impressive than a productized catalog.

Pros

  • Feels strict and enterprise-safe
  • Very readable for technical buyers

Tradeoff

C. Layered Platform Map

Shows the services as a system with explicit operating layers. Best at platform storytelling, but individual services become slightly less distinct.

Pros

  • Explains architecture fast
  • Feels strategic and enterprise-grade

Tradeoff

  • Service-level discoverability drops a bit
My recommendation

Pick A if you want users to immediately feel “this is a serious enterprise product with distinct services.” Pick C only if you care more about platform narrative than service-by-service clarity.
